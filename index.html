Who We Are

About Us

Toronto Downtown Islamic Society (TDIS) was founded by Muslims who live, work, and pray in the western part of downtown Toronto. We are not an organization that started in a boardroom. We started on a sidewalk.

Like many Muslims in this area, we found ourselves with no dedicated place to pray. No masjid. No prayer space. Nothing within reach. And as the western downtown core continues to grow at one of the fastest rates in the entire country, with new towers rising every year and thousands of new residents arriving, that gap only gets wider.

We looked around, spoke to others, and quickly realized we were not alone in this struggle. So we did something about it.

TDIS was established in Ramadan 1447 / 2026 as a registered nonprofit under the Ontario Not-for-Profit Corporations Act (ONCA), built on one simple belief: this community deserves a home.

What We Are Building

Our Goals

01
Prayer Space

A Dedicated Prayer Space

Our primary goal is to establish a permanent, accessible prayer space for the Muslims of downtown Toronto West — a place for daily prayers, Jumu'ah, Taraweeh, and community gatherings. A place that is always open. A place that feels like home.

This is not just a convenience. For a community this size, in a city of this stature, it is a necessity.

02
Dawah & Quran

Dawah and Free Quran Distribution

We believe a masjid in the heart of downtown Toronto is more than a prayer space — it is an opportunity. Downtown Toronto has one of the highest concentrations of people with no religious affiliation in the country. We see that not as a challenge, but as a calling.

TDIS is actively making dawah and distributing free Qurans near Wellington Place in downtown Toronto. This is just the beginning.
